Previous Reads
The Butlerian Jihad
by Brian Herbert Kevin J. Anderson

As I've said before , my experience has been that a book of 500 pages is a sure sign that there was not a strong editor to trim the bloat. This book is another example of that. The pacing is poor, the style is a Grisham-esque three page chapter construction and the insights into human/robot interactions and relations are sophmoric. Stanislaw Lem did a better job 40 years. All in all, a long read and a work that would have been handled better with better writing, a more thoughtful insight into the complexities of the world and a deep creation of that world. (October 2003)
The Kiln People
by David Brin

My past experience has been that a book of 500 pages is a sign that there was not a strong editor to trim the bloat. This book is an example of that. The first half of this book is fantastic -- the inventiveness and detail is unbelievable. David Brin created a deeply detailed and believable world for this story and he pulls it off every well. The ending peters out, though, and the 500 page problem leads to 100+ pages of petering. Oh, and the basic plot is a detective story but the ending is a "pull a rabbit out of the hat" type solution rather than any real mystery. Sigh, a sad thing all around. (May 2003)
Confessions of an Ugly Stepsister
by Gregory Maguire Bill Sanderson (illustrator)

This is a fun read with good characters and the ending is much better than Wicked: The Life and Times of the Wicked Witch of the West . This is the Cinderalla story told by one of the ugly stepsisters. Maguire builds a believable story around the fairy tail explaining how the father of the beautiful girl looses a wife, marries an "evil stepmother" and then disappears from the scene letting the story of the ball play out. And what is "happily ever after". A good book. (May 2003)
Beyond Flesh
by Jack Dann (editor) Gardner Dozois (editor)

Normally I'd give a science fiction collection of short stories a 3-head rating because I really like that sort of thing. But this was very disappointing. These editors seem to but together books on different themes by picking short stories from the many science fiction publications that specialize in that (Analog, ...). This collection contained stories that speculated about a world after people moved beyond our current fleshy container. The 2-head rating is not for the stories some of which are very creating, entertaining and well written. This collection, though, is very weak with some stories only partually connected to the theme of the book. While some of the individual short stories are great, this collection is a hack job. (January 2003)
